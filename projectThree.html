Refood

Refood is an application(prototype) that allows people to track their food waste and keep them informed about the problem. I was in a team of three and we had to work with wicked problems. Refood is one solution based on creating values for a group of users instead of functionalities.

How did it started?

One of our school projects was to choose and work with an open problem issue(wicked problems). We had to provide a solution by applying a user-centered approach(Design thinking), focusing more on group values than functionalities.

Overview

For our project, my team agreed to work on the problem - food waste as it is affecting everyone, despite geography, gender, or social status. It is an issue that occurs in all phases in the lifecycle of an organic product.

My role

I was part of a team of three and we were trying to solve the food waste problem, applying a user-centered approach focusing on user values. My role in the project included research, project planning, testing, and designing.

Client

none

Time

3 Weeks

Research

User Interviews

For this project, we conducted seven user interviews in order to get a better understanding of the problem. Ten to fifteen minutes each.

What did we got?

  • Our target audience is quite busy.

  • People are bothered by the problem but they do not know how can help.

  • We noticed that the people express strong emotions when we represent the date of how much food is wasted by a person.

Information Architecture

AI

From our research, we gathered that our target audience is rather busy and we had to come up with a product that they can use in their daily life. We conducted several tests in order to gather information on what functionalities we should have that it will make the app applicable.

Sitemaps

With the Affinity diagram method, we were able to see how the content will be spread out through the website, how it will be prioritized, and what elements be displayed. After that, we used Sitemaps to create a model of a website's structure and content presenting the navigation between the pages.

Design Phase

Sketches

With the first sketches, I tried to work with the content. The main problem here was that the content was too much and spread out on different pages. So, I had to create a layout that had to combine all the content in one page using different theories and methods such as Gestalt principles.

Wireframes

To become more concrete with our design, we created medium-fidelity wireframes. We are specifying depictions of the layout, we are putting emphasis on particular elements, we are arranging text, but still, we are not using final font style, spacing, images, and colors.

Usability Testing

As we have finished our mockups, we performed several Think aloud tests from which observe the user behaviors and relation with our design, navigation, and content. We performed 5 Think aloud test with our Figma mockups- mobile version of the app.

Final Product

What was changed?

From our testing, we concluded that the tracked content was a bit stuffed with bad visibility. Also, the activity status didn't bring enough information on how can be used. Our solution was to show one tracker which shows the average food waste per capita - per week and to remove the activity status in order to increase the clarity and improve the user experince.

What have I learned?

What I have learned during this project is how to prioritize the customer's needs above all else. Also, how to apply the Design thinking(user-centered approach).
