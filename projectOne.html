Aalborg 89ers

Aalborg 89ers is an American football club. Their website is poorly structured and this makes difficult for users to navigate around and find the information they need. I was part of a team of four and we worked on organizing the content, creating a user-centered design to provide better user experiences.

How did it started?

For our exam in the second semester, we had to find a client who has design problems with their website. The Local American Football team website had poor Information Architecture and not a sufficient way of presenting the information to the different user groups.

Overview

The Aalborg’s local American Football Club has a website in which the information is scattered all over the website. The club wants to send a message that the sport is for everyone, despite gender, age, or skill level. As American football is not that popular sport in Denmark, the client wants to introduce what it is to be part of such a team and culture.

My role

I was part of a team of four and we worked on organizing the content, creating a user-centered design to provide better user experiences. My role in the project included everything from project planning, user research, design, testing, and frontend.

Client

Local American Football Club
- Aalborg 89ers.

Time

4 Weeks

Research

User Interviews

For this project, we conducted seven user interviews in order to get a better understanding of the problem. Fifteen to twenty minutes each.

What did we got?

  • It is important to the people who are going to visit the website to get the right information.

  • For parents who look for information about their children, it is important to get practical information such as schedule, fees, contact details and potential injuries.

  • The reputation/name of the brand and the recommendations were also important.

Information Architecture

AI

We conducted a Card sorting test in order to create our categories that will bring the most sense to the people. This type of testing – open card sorting, will help us see which topics according to our users are connected and it will also help us to come up with the names for the created groups.

Sitemaps

With the Affinity diagram method, we were able to see how the content will be spread out through the website, how it will be prioritized, and what elements be displayed. After that, we used Sitemaps to create a model of a website's structure and content presenting the navigation between the pages.

Design Phase

Sketches

With the first sketches, I tried to work with the content. The main problem here was that the content was too much and spread out on different pages. So, I had to create a layout that had to combine all the content in one page using different theories and methods such as Gestalt principles.

Wireframes

To become more concrete with our design, we created medium-fidelity wireframes. We are specifying depictions of the layout, we are putting emphasis on particular elements, we are arranging text, but still, we are not using final font style, spacing, images, and colors.

Usability Testing

As we have finished our mockups, we performed several Think aloud tests from which observe the user behaviors and relation with our design, navigation, and content. We performed 5 Think aloud test with our Adobe Xd mockups- both mobile and web versions of the pages.

Final Product

What was changed?

The alignment of the headings on all pages to accommodate for distinguishing design between similar pages. Also, the naming of the category INJURIES was unclear for the users and we had to change it to POTENTIAL INJURIES according to our testing. Moreover, we changed the layout on the POTENTIAL INJURIES page to increase the user experience by maximizing readability and visibility.

What have I learned?

I really enjoyed working on this project. What I learned the hard way was that I had to take the content into consideration. Because it's time-wasting to change the layout a few days before the deadline.
