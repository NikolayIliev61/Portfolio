How did it started?

For our exam in the first semester, we had chosen client which was Svalengarden Mest. They had two business models, B2B and B2C, which contains a very large amount of information and a large number of categories and subcategories. Our task was focused on the B2C part and create a design following the requirements of the client.

Overview

Svalegaarden-mest.dk is a caterer serving B2B and B2C, residing in Hasseris, Aalborg. They provide catering/canteen solutions for businesses, as well as catering to private social functions and more. Both business models are currently gathered within the same webpage, and our goal is to create a separate website for the private B2C model, in order to give the users a better experience.

My role

I worked in a team of four where our goal was to provide Svalgarden Mest with an updated design of their website which can lead to a better user experience. My role in the project included everything from project planning, user research, design, testing, and frontend.

Client

Svalegarden Mest

Time

4 Weeks

Research

User Interviews

For this project, we conducted six user interviews in order to get a better understanding of the problem. Fifteen to twenty minutes each.

What did we got?

  • The quality of the food as well as how it looks was of importance.

  • The positive words and recommendations mattered.

  • Communication and feeling of inclusiveness in the process of organizing and decorating their events were significant.

Information Architecture

AI

The customer product had poor Information Architecture and most of the information was spread out on different pages which were confusing for the users. Based on the user research and the content inventory, we improved the content by putting it all together instead of spreading it. Also, we changed some of the categories into more proper ones and added the Reviews section on the main page. All of this brought better user experience and clarity.

Sitemaps

With the Affinity diagram method, we were able to see how the content will be spread out through the website, how it will be prioritized, and what elements be displayed. After that, we used Sitemaps to create a model of a website's structure and content presenting the navigation between the pages.

Design Phase

Sketches

After the analysis of the user research and the standard data, we decided to go with the mobile-first approach. In order to represent the four seasons in a visible and readable way we had to, create 4 squares which each of them representing a different season with its color. The most common layout, showing each section one under another wouldn't have worked for us because if it's summer the user had to scroll to the bottom of the website to find the menu while now he can just see it on the top.

Wireframes

To become more concrete with our design, we created medium-fidelity wireframes. We are specifying depictions of the layout, we are putting emphasis on particular elements, we are arranging text, but still, we are not using final font style, spacing, images, and colors.

Usability Testing

As we have finished our mockups, we performed several Think aloud tests from which observe the user behaviors and relation with our design, navigation, and content. We performed 5 Think aloud test with our Adobe Xd mockups- both mobile and web versions of the pages.

Final Product

What was changed?

From the testing, we received that we had to create a better contrast in order to put more attention to the content. That's why we changed the white background to a darker one. Also, we make the seasonal menu in different colors which represents the seasons for a better user experience. The alignment of the titles and the text was changed a bit in some places to create a better balance on the page.

What have I learned?

Reflecting back on the project, we have learned a lot about utilizing different theories and methods to make us find solution to problems that are relevant to specific target audience. What I learned the hard way was that I had to take the content into consideration. Because it's time-wasting to change the layout a few days before the deadline.
